Basic information of Korea

capital

Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is an international metropolis with about one-fifth of South Korea's total population. The official name is Seoul Special City.
Population: 10 million people Area: 605 square kilometers

The old name of Seoul has changed to Hanyang, Hanseong, and Gyeongseong and is now Seoul.
Hanyang is a place name that has been used since the Silla era and was named to mean "land on the north side of the Han River" because "yang" means the north side of the river. After that, it was renamed Hanseong in 1395 after the founding of Korea. The name Gyeongseong was used between 1910 and 1945 during the Japanese colonial era. And the name Seoul currently used means "capital" in Korean.
Today, Seoul is Korea's No. 1 tourist city with everything from delicious food, shopping, and cosmetics.

climate

The average annual temperature in Seoul is 12.9 ℃.
There hasn't been much precipitation. And there is a continental climate.

Since the latitude of Seoul is almost the same as that of Niigata City, the climate is similar to that of Niigata City.
In Seoul, where the four seasons are clear, the summer is extremely hot and it may rain, but on the other hand, the cold winter is severe and it does not rain much.

geography

Seoul has a basin structure surrounded by mountains and hills with an altitude of around 500m, including Mt. Bukhan.

The terrain that prevents attacks from such foreign enemies is also one of the reasons why Seoul has developed since ancient times.
